Eiffel Tower
Paris, France

The Eiffel Tower, or La Tour Eiffel in French, is one of the most well-known landmarks in the world. The tower was built as the centerpiece of the 1889 World's Fair in Paris, with the goal of commemorating the French Revolution's centennial and showcasing France's modern technological supremacy on a global scale. The mission has been completed. Gustave Eiffel's civil engineering business used 7,500 tons of iron and 2.5 million rivets to construct the tower in two years, two months, and five days. The final product of Eiffel's labors dominates the Parisian skyline, and its stark silhouette has been imitated all over the world, including China, Las Vegas, Greece, and, of course, Paris, Texas.
