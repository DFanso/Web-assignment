Pyramids of Giza
Egypt

The Great Pyramid of Giza (also known as the Pyramid of Khufu or the Pyramid of Cheops) is the oldest and largest of the Giza pyramid complex, which borders modern-day Giza in Egypt. It is the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, as well as the only one that has been preserved in its entirety. Egyptologists believe the pyramid was built as a tomb for Egyptian pharaoh Khufu of the Fourth Dynasty, and that it was constructed over a 27-year period in the 26th century BC. For more than 3,800 years, the Great Pyramid was the world's highest man-made edifice, standing at 146.5 meters (481 ft). The smooth white limestone casing was gradually removed, lowering the pyramid's height to the current 138.5 meters (454.4 ft). The basic core structure is what can be observed now.
