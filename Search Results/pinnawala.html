Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage
Sri Lanka

Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage (Sinhala: ) is a wild Asian elephant orphanage, nursery, and captive breeding site located in Pinnawala village, Sabaragamuwa Province, Sri Lanka, 13 kilometers (8.1 miles) northeast of Kegalle town. Pinnawala boasts the world's largest captive elephant herd. In 2011, there were 96 elephants, including 43 males and 68 females from 3 generations, residing in Pinnawala. The orphanage was established to care for and safeguard the many orphaned and unweaned wild elephants found wandering around Sri Lanka's forests. The Sri Lanka Department of Wildlife Conservation founded it in 1975. (DWC).
