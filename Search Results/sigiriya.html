Sigiriya
Sri Lanka

Sigiriya or Sinhagiri (Lion Rock in Sinhala, Tamil: /, pronounced see-gi-ri-y) is a historic rock stronghold in Sri Lanka's Central Province. It is located in the northern Matale District near the town of Dambulla. It is a historically and archaeologically significant site that is dominated by a gigantic granite column that stands 180 meters (590 feet) tall. [2] According to the Culavamsa, this location was once a vast forest, but following storms and landslides, it was transformed into a hill and chosen by King Kashyapa (477 â€“ 495 AD) as his new capital. On top of this rock, he erected his palace, which was adorned with colorful frescoes on all sides. He erected a doorway in the shape of a huge lion on a little plateau approximately halfway up the side of this rock. Snhgiri, or Lion Rock, is the name given to this location because of its construction (an etymology similar to Sinhapura, the Sanskrit name of Singapore, the Lion City).
