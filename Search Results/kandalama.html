Kandalama
Sri Lanka

Kandalama is a Sri Lankan village. It is found in the province of Central. The 1st Century BC Dambulla cave temple and the 5th Century AD Sigiriya rock fortification are both UNESCO world heritage sites in Kandalama. Kandalama is also home to the Kaludiya Pokuna Archeological Forest and Heritance Kandalama, one of Sri Lanka's first five star hotels, from which you may admire the majesty of two UNESCO world heritage sites: Dambulla Cave Temple and Sigiriya Rock Fortress. Kandalama is home to an old irrigation tank created by King Vasabha around 1700 years ago. The village is located at the northern end of the Matale hills and the beginning of the dry plains, at the foot of Ereulagala 696m (above M.S.L) and Dikkandahena 618m (above M.S.L). The settlement is situated on the dividing line between the wet and dry zones.
