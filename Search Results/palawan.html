Palawan Island
Philippines

Palawan is the largest island in the Philippines' province of Palawan, as well as the country's fifth-largest by area and tenth-most populous island, with a population of 994,101 as of the 2020 census. The island's north west coast runs along the South China Sea, while its south east coast forms part of the Sulu Sea's northern boundary.Â Much of the island is still traditional and underdeveloped, according to some. Many visitors, as well as multinational firms searching for development prospects, are drawn to the area by the abundance of wildlife, rainforest mountains, and some white sand beaches.
